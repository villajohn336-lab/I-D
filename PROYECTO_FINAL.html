Final Project

Anti-Sleep Band

The Smart Drowsiness Alert System is a wearable device designed to help prevent traffic accidents caused by driver fatigue. Using motion sensors and an intelligent electronic system, the prototype detects abnormal head movements and activates visual and audible alerts to warn the user, integrating mechanical design, electronics, programming, and 3D printing into a functional solution.

Stage 01

Concept Development and Preliminary Design

The project began by evaluating different ideas focused on solving a real-world problem through digital fabrication technologies. After the analysis, the Smart Drowsiness Alert Band was selected as the most feasible and impactful proposal.

Next, the first sketches of the device were created, and the required materials and electronic components were selected, establishing the foundation for the following stages of the project.

Stage 02

PCB Design and Footprint Development

Once the concept and components were defined, the electronic circuit was designed in KiCad. The schematic was created by connecting each component according to the planned functionality of the Anti-Sleep Band.

After completing the schematic, the PCB layout was developed by arranging the components and routing the electrical connections to create a compact and functional printed circuit board.

Custom footprints were reviewed and adjusted to ensure that every electronic component matched its physical dimensions and could be correctly assembled on the PCB.

This stage made it possible to verify the board layout, optimize the available space, and prepare the design for the manufacturing process.

Footprint

PCB Design Result

The completed PCB design served as the foundation for the manufacturing stage, ensuring that all components were correctly positioned and connected before fabrication.

Stage 03

Breadboard Testing

Before manufacturing the PCB, the complete electronic circuit was assembled and tested on a breadboard. This stage allowed the team to verify the electrical connections and ensure that each component worked correctly.

The breadboard tests helped identify and correct possible wiring or programming issues before moving to the fabrication stage, reducing errors during the final assembly.

Stage 04

PCB Fabrication and Drilling

Once the PCB design was completed, the circuit was transferred onto a copper-clad board to begin the manufacturing process. This stage transformed the digital design into a physical printed circuit board.

After printing the circuit, the board was carefully drilled to create the mounting holes for the electronic components, preparing it for the assembly stage.

Stage 05

Component Soldering and Circuit Assembly

This stage focuses on the soldering and assembly of the electronic components onto the PCB. The process is currently being repeated to improve the quality of the connections and ensure the correct operation of the circuit.

Once the soldering process is completed, the board will become the main electronic core of the Anti-Sleep Band and will be integrated into the 3D-printed enclosure for final functional testing.

Laboratory Practices 2026

Reports, experiments and innovation projects developed during laboratory practices.

Practice 1 • Week 1

3D printed keychain

A class was held to familiarize the student with the operation of the printers available in the laboratory, seeking to stimulate creativity.

1. Keyring sketch

Working groups were formed to design the keychains according to the students' tastes, selecting sports t-shirts in this case.

2. Digitization in Fusion 360

Once the idea was defined, the Fusion tool was used to create both the 2D and 3D design of the keychain, giving it the desired dimensions.

3. Programming in CURA

The file is exported in STL format so it can be imported into Cura and the printing parameters defined. It's important to consider the material, the printing speed, and whether supports are needed. There are many more factors, but those mentioned are the most important.

4. 3D printing

The keychain was printed in generic PLA on a PRUSA printer, with an estimated printing time of around 15 minutes per keychain. It should be noted that other factors were taken into account during printing, such as ensuring the machine was properly calibrated, preheating it, and verifying that the filament was correctly installed.

Practice 1 • Week 2

Water Bottle Design

Following the scientific research carried out in Assignment 1 week 2, the implementation of the product that was considered suitable to solve the problem had to be carried out, so a series of fundamental steps were followed for this.

1. Sketch
Based on the statistical analysis previously carried out, it was found that the points of greatest interest for university students are the functionality and comfort of the product, so a design was sought based on the aforementioned.
2. Design in Fusion 360
Once the design was defined, the digital tracing began in the Fusion 360 application. This is a practical and easy-to-use tool for computer-aided design.
3. Parameterization
When modeling in Fusion, it is important to keep in mind that the design must be parameterized; this will allow it to be adapted to any change in size without having to restart the entire drawing process from scratch.
4. Extrusion
Finally, the figure must be extruded to create the 3D model and better visualize its appearance. This also includes incorporating elements such as the threads on the cap and the bottle body.
Practice 3 • Weeks 4 and 5

3D Scanning, Resin Printing and Mold Making

This laboratory practice covered the complete process of converting a real person into a three-dimensional model. The work included 3D scanning, digital model correction, resin printing, post-processing, mold design and the production of a final wax figure.

01

3D Scanning with CR Studio

A group member was scanned using a 3D scanner and CR Studio. The process was performed slowly, with the lights turned off and the person's hair covered to improve the capture of facial and body details. Several angles were recorded to create a complete digital model, which was repaired and exported in OBJ format.

02

Model Optimization in Blender

The OBJ file was imported into Blender to correct imperfections created during scanning. Rough surfaces were smoothed, holes were repaired and the mesh was closed to obtain a completely solid model. A flat base was also created before exporting the corrected design in STL format.

03

Resin Printing and Post-Processing

The STL file was imported into Chitubox, where the model was hollowed with a wall thickness of 3 mm. Drainage holes and automatic supports were added before preparing the file for the Anycubic Photon Max Plus printer. After printing, the supports were removed, the piece was washed and it received ultraviolet curing for 20 minutes.

04

Mold Design and Final Wax Casting

A two-part mold was designed in Blender using cubes and Boolean operations to reproduce the front and back of the figure. A cylindrical channel was added to pour the material. The mold was exported in STL format and printed with flexible TPU. Finally, melted wax was poured into the mold, cooled for approximately 30 minutes and carefully removed.

Main Results

Knowledge and skills developed

01

A real person was successfully transformed into a printable three-dimensional digital model.

02

Blender made it possible to repair the geometry and improve the quality of the scanned model.

03

Orientation, exposure time, layer height and supports were identified as essential printing parameters.

04

The final mold allowed the digital model to be reproduced as a physical wax figure.

!

Safety Considerations

Nitrile gloves, safety glasses, protective clothing, closed footwear and respiratory protection were used during the practice. Resin and isopropyl alcohol were handled in a ventilated area and away from heat or ignition sources.
